## Tokenomics Overview

Bitcoin's tokenomics score of 79/100 reflects what is categorically the most credible supply model in digital assets. The 4th halving (April 2024) reduced block rewards to 3.125 BTC/block — ~450 BTC issued daily at roughly $35M/day, with inflation at ~0.85% annually. Over 95.4% of the 21M supply is already circulating; the remaining ~980,000 BTC will be mined over the next ~120 years via geometrically declining issuance. There are no vesting schedules, no investor unlocks, and no governance risk of supply expansion. The long-term holder (LTH) cohort now represents a record 33%+ of supply dormant for 5+ years — a structural supply squeeze. ETF inflows in April alone ($2.44B) significantly exceeded total new BTC mined that month, creating an unambiguous net supply deficit.

The primary tokenomics risk is the long-run security budget: as block subsidies decline toward zero (5th halving ~2028, 6th ~2032), Bitcoin's network security must increasingly rely on transaction fee revenue. Current fees represent only 5–15% of miner income and have recently hit 12-month lows — the fee market is not yet deep enough to sustain security at sub-reward levels without sustained price appreciation or fee demand growth. Satoshi's ~1.1M dormant BTC (~5.5% of supply) is an irreducible tail risk with no conventional finance equivalent.
